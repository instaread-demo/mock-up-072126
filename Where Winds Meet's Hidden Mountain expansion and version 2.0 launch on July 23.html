Prinny Party: Going Overboard is a new party game featuring Disgaea characters coming to consoles and PC

NIS America has announced Prinny Party: Going Overboard, a new party game featuring Disgaea characters, for PS5, Switch, Switch 2, and PC (Steam).

Prinny Party: Going Overboard features dual audio, English subtitles, and it will also feature Steam Remote Play Together and GameShare support on Switch 2. Note that it does not have online multiplayer support in a traditional sense, and only supports multiplayer online through those features. You can find the Prinny Party: Going Overboard announcement trailer with more details from NIS America below:

NIS America, Inc. is excited to announce that Prinny Party: Going Overboard! is coming to Nintendo Switch 2, Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 5, and Steam.

In Prinny Party: Going Overboard!, a 4-player party game, you and your friends will do whatever it takes to defeat the Supreme Overboard and his Protago-Knights! In practice, it's quite simple: move around the board, earn experience, acquire more powerful equipment, and turn in quests to stay ahead of the curve. Then, when a boss spawns, work together to take him down!

But friendships don't last long in Prinny Party. Only the player who brings the boss back to spawn gets the credit, so do whatever it takes to be the hero!

Throw, fight, and squabble your way to victory! Whatever you do, just don't die all at once, or it'll be game over—for everyone!

Features:

  • Huck, Chuck, Boom!: Pick up and throw your friends, enemies, and frenemies to put things in their place. As always, be careful when handling Prinnies!
  • Build a Better Tomorrow: By landing on a build space, you can build and upgrade facilities, such as hospitals or armories. Just remember: other players will be able to shop there, too!
  • It Pays to Pass Some Bills: Triple your damage, move again, or build a cannon capable of wiping out even the most powerful demons. Everything is possible when you pass bills at the Assembly! 

Prinny Party: Going Overboard! features GameShare, so you can play with your friends even if they don't own a copy of the game!
